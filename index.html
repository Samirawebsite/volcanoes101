What are volcanoes?

A volcano is an opening in the earth's crust through which lava, volcanic ash, and gasses escape. Volcanic eruptions are partly driven by pressure from dissolved gas. Beneath a volcano, liquid magma containing dissolved gasses rises through cracks in the Earth's crust. As the magma rises, pressure decreases, allowing the gasses to form bubbles. (Government of Canada, 2024)
